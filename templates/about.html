AVW Dashboard

The Active Video Watching Dashboard is a tool built to enable quick and easy analysis of learner activity data. This data is from the log files produced by the Active Video Watching environments which is described below. The rationale is simple:
  • A tutor can identify good and bad learning behaviours
  • A tutor may not be skilled to analyse the log data and derive necessary insight
  • A data analyst may not have the desired background knowledge to note the truly important trends and signals from their analysis
  • Provide an easy analysis tool for the tutor to explore the data themselves and notice iimportant patterns which ONLY they can because of their domain knowledge

What is AVW?

The Active Video Watching system (AVW) is a tool which was built by a team of researchers to aid both tutors and students in their video-based learning approach. It allows tutors to add some form of engagement to videos (including publicly sourced videos e.g. from Youtube) and present them to students in a classroom. Reflective Experiential learning (i.e. cognition through reflection on past experiences in contrast with present information) of soft skills like collaboration, critical thinking and inter-cultural awareness, is one of the main purposes of AVW.

The way the system works is simple and the following description would help provide a more lucid image. The tutor gathers or creates some videos relevant to the subject to be taugt. The tutor then creates a class with those videos after adding descriptions and aspects to the videos. The aspects are supposed to draw the learner's attention to important points in the video and trigger reflection i.e. to get the learner dwelling on certain concepts and recalling past experiences as well.

The learning environment has both a Personal Space and a Social Space. In the personal space, each individual learner can watch the videos; pause at any point, enter their thought/comment in a textbox provided and annotate the comment with any of the aspects. The comment is saved once they resume watching the video.

The social space contains all comments from all learners which have been approved and and anonymized by the tutor. Each learner can view comments from other learners and rate them (replies are currently not supported, so this differs from a discussion forum). The learners can also view the video snippet related to the comment made by other learners in the social space. The tutor can download the interaction log as an XML file to be used further analysis and this file is the main raw material for this dashboard tool.

THE EXPERIMENT

The data which was used to drive the visualizations in this dashboard were from an experiment conducted using AVW to teach Pitch Presentation skills to a group of Postgraduate students. A total of 8 videos were carefully selected from Youtube; 4 were tutorials of how to give presentations (labelled 'TUTORIAL') and the other 4 were actual presentations (labelled 'EXAMPLE'). The students were made to watch the TUTORIAL videos and then the EXAMPLE videos.

The ASPECTS used for the TUTORIAL videos to stimulate learners to recall and reflect on their past experiences are:

  • I am rather good at this
  • I did/saw this in the past
  • I didn't realize I wasn't doing it
  • I like this point
The ASPECTS used for the EXAMPLE videos to help learners reinforce concepts taught in the tutorial videos are:
  • Delivery
  • Visual aids
  • Structure
  • Speech

The final part of the experiment was getting the learners to use the social space. They were able to view the comments of other learners and rate those comments using the following available options which were also carefully selected to foster even deeper reflection. The RATING OPTIONS were:

  • This is useful for me
  • I hadn't thought of this
  • I didn't notice this
  • I don't agree with this
  • I like this point
The first three (3) were termed the LEARNING TRIGGER RATINGS while the last two were called OPINION INDUCING RATINGS.

The other parts of the experiment are not relevant here, as the data from those parts were not used in preparing this dashboard. The interaction data based on the above explanation was used solely for the dashboard.
